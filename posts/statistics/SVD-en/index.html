2026-02-04

SVD and QR Decomposition

SVD and QR decomposition are used in AI compilers for weight compression and compute optimization analysis.

  1. QR Decomposition QR decomposition represents a matrix AAA as a product of an orthogonal matrix QQQ and an upper triangular matrix RRR.

The formula is A=QRA = QRA=QR

  • QQQ (Orthogonal Matrix): column vectors are mutually orthogonal and have unit length. (QTQ=IQ^TQ = IQTQ=I)
    • Orthogonal means the inner product of two vectors is 0.
  • RRR (Upper Triangular Matrix): all entries below the diagonal are 0.
  • Use cases: solving linear systems, least squares, eigenvalue computation (QR algorithm).
  • Meaning
  • It formalizes the Gram-Schmidt process.
  • Since orthogonalization reduces numerical instability, it is often used to solve Ax=bAx = bAx=b in systems requiring high precision.
  1. SVD (Singular Value Decomposition) SVD decomposes any m×nm \times nm×n matrix AAA into a product of three matrices, and is one of the most powerful tools in linear algebra.

The formula is A=UΣVTA = U\Sigma V^TA=UΣVT

  • UUU (Left Singular Matrix): an m×mm \times mm×m orthogonal matrix of eigenvectors of AATAA^TAAT.
  • Σ\SigmaΣ (Singular Value Matrix): an m×nm \times nm×n diagonal (rectangular) matrix with singular values σi=λi\sigma_i = \sqrt{\lambda_i}σi​=λi​​ on the diagonal, sorted in descending order.
  • VVV (Right Singular Matrix): an n×nn \times nn×n orthogonal matrix of eigenvectors of ATAA^TAATA.
  • Meaning
  • SVD decomposes a matrix into the process of "rotation (VTV^TVT) -> scaling (Σ\SigmaΣ) -> rotation (UUU)".
  • Removing small singular values enables strong performance for data compression (PCA) and denoising.
  1. Points to Think About
  • Why compute ATAA^TAATA in SVD?
    • The key is understanding how matrix AAA transforms space.
    • ATAA^TAATA is symmetric, so it always has orthogonal eigenvectors (VVV).
    • These eigenvectors define the principal axes before the transformation by AAA, and the eigenvalues indicate how much each axis is stretched (σ2\sigma^2σ2).
  • Why is QR decomposition preferred over SVD for solving linear systems?
    • SVD typically requires iterative numerical methods to find eigenvalues, which is expensive.
    • QR decomposition can be computed in finite steps via Gram-Schmidt or Householder transformations, so it is much faster.
